Save the Roaches of Uganda

🪳
A displaced cockroach surveys what remains of its habitat. Photo: Field team, undisclosed location.

They have survived 300 million years, two mass extinctions, and every shoe ever thrown. But they have never faced a threat like indifference.

Across Uganda, the common cockroach — nature's most resilient and most misunderstood resident — is being overlooked by every major conservation fund on earth. Pandas get the cameras. Whales get the documentaries. Nobody is standing up for the little guy with six legs and a dream.

That changes today. The Roach Resilience Initiative is mobilizing on the ground to provide shelter, dignity, and a future for thousands of Uganda's most enduring insects.

"If anyone built this, I'd have no choice. I'd have to donate. It's just who I am." — the man this campaign was lovingly built for

Where your money goes

Every dollar funds critical roach infrastructure: artisanal cardboard housing, premium crumb redistribution, and a 24-hour emergency hotline for roaches in crisis. A donation of just $20,000 fully funds the flagship sanctuary.

You will not get a tax receipt. You will not get a tote bag. You will get the quiet, lifelong knowledge that when the roaches needed you, you showed up.
